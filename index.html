The Industry Problem

A £326 trillion market
running on fragmented chaos.

Real estate is the world's largest asset class — yet less than 5% of property transactions and management workflows are fully digitised. The infrastructure was never built. Until now.

Problem 01
⬡

Siloed Roles, Zero Shared Data

Landlords, agents, tenants, contractors, lawyers, accountants — each in disconnected tools with no shared source of truth. A single property can involve 8–12 separate systems. Every handoff is a point of friction and data loss.

Problem 02
⬡

No Trust or Verification Layer

Unlike financial services (credit scores) or transport (driver ratings), property has no universal trust system. A rogue contractor re-enters under a new name. A bad tenant disappears. This costs the UK economy an estimated £2.7 billion annually in disputes and remediation.

Problem 03
⬡

50+ Provider Categories Without a Business OS

Tradespeople, engineers, suppliers, transporters, estate agents, lawyers, and accountants each operate in professional silos — no shared data, no unified business intelligence, no certification ecosystem, and no channel to market at scale.

Multi-Directional Review System

PropOS reviews are transaction-verified only (no transaction = no review) and mutual & simultaneous (neither party sees the other's review until both submit). Nine review directions across all role combinations.

Trigger: Move-out + deposit settled
Landlord reviews Tenant

Rent paid on time, property condition, communication quality, vacated cleanly, would re-let to. Visible only to other landlords and agents when tenant applies — not general public.

Protection: Tenants cannot be disadvantaged for leaving negative reviews. Any landlord action (rent increase, S21) within 90 days of negative review triggers automatic compliance flag.
Trigger: Tenancy ends + deposit resolved
Tenant reviews Landlord

Repairs response speed, communication quality, property condition at move-in, deposit returned fairly, would rent from again. Visible to prospective tenants and agents.

Impact: Good landlords attract better tenants. Bad landlords face reduced demand. Tenant power built into the platform infrastructure.
Trigger: Work order signed off as complete
Manager/Owner reviews Contractor

Quality of work, punctuality, quote accuracy, communication, would rehire. Visible to all owners and managers searching for contractors — summary visible on marketplace.

+ Supplier reviews: Contractors also review suppliers on delivery accuracy, product quality, and price. Helps other contractors find reliable materials partners.
Trigger: Invoice confirmed as paid
Contractor reviews Owner/Manager

Payment speed, clear job instructions, property access ease, reasonable expectations, would work for again. Good contractors are selective about clients — this gives them market power.

Critical: This is the only platform where good contractors can signal which clients they prefer — creating competitive pressure on landlords and managers to treat providers fairly.
